## Design Context

### Users
SME owners and finance managers in Zambia — typically a business owner with 5–50 employees who currently runs payroll in Excel, makes bank transfers one by one, and manually reconciles payments each month cycle. They're on desktop in an office or at home; many are also mobile. They're not deeply technical. They feel the pain of payroll season — stressed, error-prone, time-consuming. When they land on the Anchor website, they need to feel immediately: *this was built for me, not for some US startup*.

### Brand Personality
**Trustworthy. Local. Warm.**

Not a Silicon Valley startup. Not a cold European fintech. Think: the best-run commercial bank branch in Lusaka — professional, confident, and the staff actually know your name. A place where your money is safe AND you feel like a person.

Anti-references: anything that looks like a generic SaaS template, anything that could be mistaken for a US or UK product.

### Brand Identity
**Real brand color is PURPLE** — `oklch(0.50 0.22 292)` — with a warm amber/gold accent (`oklch(0.80 0.14 68)`). The logo files are in `public/` (logo-mark.png, logo-full.png, logo-black.png). Use `brightness-0 invert` CSS filter on dark backgrounds to show the logo white.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Institutional confidence with human warmth.**

- **Theme**: Light mode. Warm cream/ivory surfaces (not cold white). Deep purple (`oklch(0.24 0.16 292)`) for hero and CTA dark sections. Amber for CTAs on dark backgrounds, brand purple for CTAs on light backgrounds.
- **Colors**: Brand purple (`--color-brand`) + warm amber accent (`--color-amber`). Purple-tinted neutral surfaces. Surface warm-cream tint. See `globals.css` for full token set.
- **Typography**: **Bitter** (slab serif, Google Fonts) for display headings. **Lexend** (humanist variable sans) for body text. This pairing: "we're serious about your money AND we're easy to talk to."
- **Layout**: Generous whitespace with intentional asymmetry. Not everything centered. Left-anchored text blocks. Sections that breathe.
- **Differentiation**: The product exists because of a uniquely Zambian problem — that gap between payroll calculation and mobile money disbursement. Visual motifs around the three mobile networks (MTN yellow, Airtel red, Zamtel green) used tastefully. "Made in Lusaka, Zambia" feels like a badge, not a disclaimer.

### Design Principles
1. **Institutional without being cold** — use slab serifs, strong contrast, and clean structure to convey authority, but warm the surfaces and use human photography to prevent clinical feeling.
2. **Zambian-first** — references to MTN, Airtel, Zamtel, NAPSA, NHIMA, Lusaka should feel natural and specific, not tacked-on. This product was built here, and the design should know it.
3. **Teal earns its keep** — accent color is reserved exclusively for primary actions and positive states (payment confirmed, payroll complete). Never decorative gradients, never background fills.
4. **Clarity over cleverness** — no metaphorical hero animations, no abstract blob shapes. The hero communicates the product in one clear sentence. Complexity is progressively revealed through scrolling.
5. **Warm surfaces, not cold whites** — all background colors carry a faint Navy-tinted warmth. Pure white (#ffffff) is never used as a surface color.
